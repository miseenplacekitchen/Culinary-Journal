DMCA & Takedown Procedure

Last updated: June 2026 · Draft for legal review

We respect intellectual property. If you believe content on The Culinary Journal infringes your copyright, send a notice with the information below.

Your notice should include

  1. Your name, address, telephone number, and email.
  2. Identification of the copyrighted work you claim is infringed.
  3. The URL or enough detail to locate the material on our site.
  4. A statement that you have a good-faith belief the use is not authorised.
  5. A statement, under penalty of perjury, that the information is accurate and you are the rights holder or authorised agent.
  6. Your physical or electronic signature.

Counter-notification

If your content was removed and you believe the removal was mistaken, you may submit a counter-notification with the same level of detail and a statement consenting to jurisdiction of the courts in your region, where applicable.

Repeat infringers

Accounts that repeatedly infringe copyright may be suspended or permanently deactivated.
